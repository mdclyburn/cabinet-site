Summary

The X-ray Cabinet is a device developed to assist with research on the effects of low doses of radiation on fibroblast cells. The Cabinet weighs about 100 pounds and is made of cold-rolled steel and aluminum. This design manual explains each component of the X-ray Cabinet in depth.

Safety Precautions

The Mini-X emits harmful radiation. Before using the X-ray Cabinet, ensure that the safety device is connected and the door is securely closed. Please refer to the safety manual to become more familiar with necessary safety precautions regarding radiation.

Exterior Part Descriptions

The door (Figure 1) is made out of steel and coated in electroless nickel to prevent rusting. It is designed to allow air to flow from outside the cabinet to inside the cabinet. However, there is no direct path for radiation to escape. The track the door slides along is made of steel to prevent wear. When opening the door, ensure that the door slides straight along the track. Do not try to remove the door, or you could damage the safety device attached to the back of the door. A standard AC power port is on the outside of the X-ray Cabinet. When plugged in, it provides power to the inner electrical box, an outer USB port, and an outer serial port (Figure 2). The serial port will connect the Mini-X to a computer equipped with the Mini-X Control Software. The USB port allows you to connect the SDD to a computer. Its design is sensitive to excessive force, so be careful when inserting the USB cord. A power switch is also on the outside of the X-ray Cabinet. Note that when the switch is on, no power will be supplied until the door is securely shut.

Interior Part Descriptions

Safety Device (Figure 4)

The safety device is comprised of two industrial-grade magnets. When the magnets are together, they will complete the circuit and power can be supplied to the contents of the X-ray Cabinet; even a slight disconnect between them will cause a break in the circuit, cutting the power for the whole cabinet.

Inner Electrical Box (Figure 5)

The inner electrical box is comprised of two electrical sockets. The case fan and the Mini-X will be plugged in here. The Mini-X will also be plugged into the USB port located on this box. There is a serial port that the detector will connect to.

Case Fan (Figure 6)

The case fan will be plugged into the inner electrical box. It draws air through the grated door and cools the Mini-X. As an additional cooling technique, there are also heat sinks that consist of blocks of aluminum next to the Mini-X. The heat sinks, combined with the case fan, ensure that the temperature inside the box remains consistent to support a stable environment for the cells.

Mini-X Mounting Plate (Figure 7)

The Mini-X Mounting Plate is a rectangular, aluminum plate. It is attached to the aluminum floor of the box at an angle. This plate can rotate thirty degrees, which is much more than needed. The plate was designed to ensure that the optimal angle could be found to maximize the amount of radiation hitting the cells.

Mini-X (Figure 7)

The Mini-X is a miniature X-ray tube that emits radiation. For more information regarding the Mini-X design, please review the Mini-X design manual. An online version can be found here: http://www.amptek.com/pdf/minix.pdf.

Mini-X Shield (Figure 8)

The Mini-X Shield prevents radiation from hitting the cells directly by guiding the beam toward the slide, and not directly at the cell culture.

Slide (Figure 9)

The Slide is the material that the radiation from the Mini-X bounces off. Research should be done to determine what material is most beneficial to irradiating the cells.

Slide Mounting Plate (Figure 9)

The Slide Mounting Plate is a rectangular, aluminum plate that is attached to the aluminum floor of the box horizontally. This mounting plate allows the slide to move up and down an inch from the center and to swivel 45 degrees either way from the horizontal. This freedom of rotation allows for adjustments to ensure that the maximum amount of radiation from the Mini-X hits the cell culture.

Cell Culture and SDD Mounting Plate (Figure 10)

The Cell Culture and SDD Mounting Plate is an L-shaped aluminum plate mounted an inch from the aluminum floor. This allows for the cell culture to fit between the slide and the floor. When the cell culture is not mounted, the SDD can be attached to the front of this plate.

Silicon Drift Detector (SDD) (Figure 11)

The SDD measures how much radiation is making contact with the cell culture. When the SDD is not in use, a protective red cap is placed over its Beryllium windows. The X-ray Cabinet is not designed to operate properly when the cap is present.

Cell Culture (Figure 12)

The Cell Culture sits on top of an L-shaped mounted slide that attaches to the Cell Culture and SDD Mounting Plate. This container is made of Mylar, which is similar to saran wrap so it does not interfere with the cells’ irradiation. The cells will be pasted onto the Mylar.

Additional Information

The X-ray Cabinet is designed to fit inside an incubator, which helps regulate the conditions for the cell culture. When the X-ray Cabinet is in the incubator, be sure to fasten it to the riser. The riser allows for the Cabinet’s door to open within the incubator. For instructions on how to setup and use the Cabinet, please refer to the instruction manual.
